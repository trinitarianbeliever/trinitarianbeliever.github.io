# The Uncreated Creator: A Trinitarian View

This article explores the concept of the Uncreated Creator from a Trinitarian perspective, refuting common anti-Trinitarian arguments.

## The Deity of Christ
The Nicene Creed affirms the divinity of Jesus Christ, stating that He is "God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God, begotten, not made, of one Being with the Father." This declaration is a cornerstone of Trinitarian theology, directly addressing early heresies that denied Christ's co-eternality and co-equality with God the Father.

### Scriptural Support
Multiple passages in the Bible support the deity of Christ. For example, John 1:1 states, "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." This verse identifies the "Word" (Jesus) as being both with God and as God Himself.
---
## The Personhood of the Holy Spirit
The Holy Spirit is not merely an impersonal force but a distinct person of the Godhead. The New Testament uses personal pronouns and attributes personal actions to the Holy Spirit, such as teaching, comforting, and testifying.
---
## Conclusion
The doctrine of the Trinity is essential for understanding the Christian faith. It reveals a God who is a community of three co-equal and co-eternal persons, united in one divine essence.
